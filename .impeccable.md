# Impeccable design context

## Design Context

### Users
Senior VC partners and analysts on the hunt for pre-deal signals. Power users who open the tool daily to scan for live, time-sensitive intelligence: a partner converging on a founder, an undisclosed Form D, a thesis shift across firms. Speed and density matter; the user is already informed, already pattern-matching. They are not browsing — they are working.

### Brand Personality
Considered. Sharp. Literary.
Voice: confident without bombast. Closer to an intelligence brief from a respected research house than to a SaaS dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refined editorial dark.** Deep warm-neutral surface (not pure black), low-contrast layering, type doing the heavy lifting. Calmer chroma than a typical dark UI — one rare warm accent used surgically, not splashed across every interactive surface. The reference points are a luxury monthly's night-mode reading view and an intelligence dossier, not a Bloomberg terminal and not a crypto dashboard.

### Anti-references
- No generic dashboard tropes: purple-blue gradients, cyan-on-dark, glow borders, glassmorphism.
- No crypto/web3 neon or mission-control decoration.
- No corporate SaaS safety: Stripe-blue, rounded mascots, Inter-on-white.
- No border-left accent stripes (impeccable ban).
- No gradient text (impeccable ban).
- No banned reflex fonts (Inter, IBM Plex *, Space *, DM *, Fraunces, Instrument *, Playfair, Outfit, Plus Jakarta, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson, Cormorant, Syne).

### Design Principles
1. **Type as instrument.** Editorial display for headlines, refined sans for body, distinctive mono for numerics. Hierarchy through size + weight, not boxes.
2. **One rare accent.** A single ember-warm hue, used as punctuation. If it appears on a button, it does not appear on a label two inches away.
3. **Density without density theatre.** Real information density where the user needs it (tables, KPIs, signal stack), generous breathing room where they don't.
4. **Tinted neutrals.** All surfaces and borders carry a faint warm hue. Nothing pure-grey, nothing pure-black.
5. **Decoration earns its place.** Every visual element must signal something — confidence, recency, ownership, status. Ornament without meaning gets cut.
